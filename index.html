## âš’ Work Experience

I've had two internships while at college: one at Optum Labs and one at Bane Welker. Both were amazing learning experiences and taught me a *ton* about developing software in a professional environment.

### Optum Labs

I worked at Optum Labs in the summer of 2022, from June-August, after my sophomore year at college. I worked on two projects at Optum Labs:

  • An automated ingestion program that downloaded survey data into a database from Qualtrics. Survey ingestion time compared to a manual process was decreased about 20x.
  • Building reusable components with React, which I tested with Jest and Playwright.

Labs was my first professional experience in the software industry, and definitely taught me some of the practices that separate professionals from amateurs, for example, storing configuration in text files, having tests associated with each pull request, and doing code reviews.

### Bane-Welker

I worked at Bane-Welker in the summer of 2023, from June-August, after my junior year at college. I worked on, and successfully deployed, three projects at Bane-Welker:

  • Two python scripts for ingesting/synchronizing parts data from one data store to another
  • A CardDav server, Radicale, for synchronizing contacts on company phones
The contacts project was a cool experience, because I'd never set up and deployed a server on my own before. We deployed it along with a python script I wrote to pull contacts from Office 365 on an AWS EC2 instance. I got ssl certificates from Let's Encrypt for free, and we got a domain name from GoDaddy. Completing this project meant that new hires could be added to Office 365 and then they'd show up as contacts on company phones with no manual intervention.
